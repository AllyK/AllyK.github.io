The Living Practice
Yoga: anatomy, philosophy & rooted teachings

The path of yoga has eight limbs of one living body that grow together. Each is a doorway into the practice. Begin anywhere.

The Eight Limbs
Guiding Principles Yama The guiding principles that root the practice, "restraints for proper behavior": non-harming, truth, non-stealing, moderation, non-grasping. Each with its sutra, its Gita root, and its stories. Enter Yama →The five principles, with the stories that teach them Surrender Niyama The discipline turns inward, ending in surrender to the divine. Here we ask what the divine means: oneness, and reciprocity among all things. Enter the Breath →Pranayama techniques Introspect Pratyahara Drawing the senses inward, away from the outer world, to find the threshold where meditation begins. Meditation →The four reaches, the inward turn Concentration Dharana Gathering the whole mind onto a single point: a flame, a gaze, a breath, a sound. The art of focus. Enter Dharana →The practices of one-pointed focus Meditation Dhyana When focus becomes effortless and unbroken, it ripens into meditation. The many doors into stillness. Meditation →Types of meditation Alchemy Samadhi The meditator and the world merge. The absolute realm of sat-chit-ananda, eternal being, non-discrimination, awareness, and bliss.
