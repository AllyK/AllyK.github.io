Dana · the practice of giving

The Living Practice is free, and it will stay free. The philosophy, the sourced stories, the pose and muscle maps, the breath and meditation guides: none of it sits behind a paywall, and none of it ever will. Most of what is here descends from a living tradition that is not mine to sell. Yama teaches asteya and aparigraha, non-stealing and non-grasping. Keeping the teachings open is part of the living practice.

What a site still needs is upkeep: a domain, a place to live on the web, and the hours it takes to write new pages, check sources, and answer the questions that come in. If the work has been useful to you and you are able to give, your gift keeps it online and growing. If you are not able to give, please use everything here freely. That is the whole point.

On Dana

Dana is the Sanskrit word for generosity, the practice of giving freely without expectation of return. In the yogic tradition, teaching was never sold. It was offered, and those who received it and could give something back did so as an act of practice in its own right. The gift kept the teaching alive and available to the next person, including the one who could not pay.

That is the model here. Think of a gift not as a price for what you have already read, which was freely given, but as a way of holding the door open behind you. Every contribution is welcomed with gratitude, and none is ever required.

Where the giving stands

This year’s upkeep

Once the year’s upkeep is met, giving does not stop mattering. It changes direction. Every gift beyond the running costs goes to two things: future trainings that let this work deepen, and lowering the cost of what Rooted Action offers, so that programming, workshops, and classes carry a smaller price and reach more people. This figure is kept honest by hand and updated as gifts arrive.

A promise, in the spirit of aparigraha: giving will never buy access to anything the rest of the site cannot reach for free. There is no members-only teaching, no locked page, no content held back for donors. A gift supports the commons. It never carves a private room inside it.
